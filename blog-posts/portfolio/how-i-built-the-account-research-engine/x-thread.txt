1/ I built a free account research engine that takes a company domain and returns a sourced brief, fit score, contacts, and a 5-touch outbound cadence.

It costs $0 to run. Here's how it works (and why the paid tools are overpriced for what they deliver):

2/ B2B contact data decays at 30-40% per year (Dun & Bradstreet). The ZoomInfo export you pulled in January is already meaningfully stale by summer.

ZoomInfo starts at $15,000/yr. You're paying a lot for data that's wrong before you import it.

3/ Gartner puts the average annual cost of dirty data at $15M per organization. Validity found 76% of orgs say less than half their CRM data is accurate.

SDRs waste 27% of their selling time chasing dead ends from that same data.

4/ So I built around the problem. The engine pulls from 5 public sources:

- Schema.org markup on the company's own site
- Wikidata (verified firmographics)
- Recent news (timing signals)
- XML sitemap (scale signals)
- Careers pages (growth signals)

5/ Schema.org is the sleeper hit here. Companies that care about SEO publish structured data that includes exactly the firmographic + product signals you need for outbound. Address, founders, pricing tiers, social profiles.

All public. All current. Zero dollars.

6/ The no-fabrication rule is the core design constraint.

The model cannot invent a number. Every quantitative claim traces to a gathered source. If the outreach wants to cite a result the seller hasn't provided, it writes a placeholder.

[INSERT RESULT FROM YOUR PORTFOLIO]

7/ Most AI research tools let the model fill gaps with "plausible" estimates. The brief sounds confident. It is often wrong in ways that are hard to detect.

A wrong brief calibrates your entire pitch around a misunderstanding. It's worse than no brief.

8/ The AI only does what AI is actually good at: classification, synthesis, tone-matching, sequencing.

Fact-gathering and fact-verification happen entirely outside the model. The model is never the source of truth. Only the interpreter of sources.

9/ Stack is boring by design:

Next.js + serverless routes
Wikidata SPARQL
Schema.org parsing
Gemini for synthesis (free tier)
Vercel hosting (free tier)

Running cost: $0. No CRM. No data vendor. No enrichment API.

10/ Yes I thought about SSRF. The system refuses private IP ranges, validates domains resolve to public addresses, rate-limits per visitor, caps input sizes, and ships a full set of security headers.

Small free tool. Same standards as a production system.

11/ What I actually learned: reps who used this said the bracketed placeholders were useful, not frustrating.

A brief that knows what it doesn't know is more valuable than one that fills every gap with a confident fabrication.

12/ McKinsey found AI can increase sales productivity by 3-5% of global sales expenditure. That number assumes AI does the tasks it's good at.

AI doing confident fabrication at scale doesn't produce productivity gains. It produces confident mistakes at scale.

13/ The engine is live. Any company domain. Brief in ~45 seconds. Sourced. No invented numbers.
