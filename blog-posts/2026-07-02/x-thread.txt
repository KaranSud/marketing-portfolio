1/ Most PLG teams are obsessing over free-to-paid conversion rates.

They're optimizing the wrong thing.

The real leak is upstream — and 40-60% of your free users are already gone before you get a chance to convert them. Thread:

2/ The industry median PLG conversion rate is 8%.

But that number is a lie. ChartMogul's data on 200 products shows a bimodal distribution: most products are either below 5% or above 15%.

If you're at 8%, you're not near average. You're in the wrong cluster.

3/ Between signup and your product's core value, something brutal happens.

40-60% of free users leave. Not because they hated the product. They never saw enough of it to form an opinion.

This is the dropout zone. It's where your growth is leaking.

4/ Userpilot's benchmark of 62 B2B SaaS companies puts the average activation rate at 37.5%.

Translation: roughly 6 out of 10 new free users disappear before any meaningful product experience. They never churned. They just never started.

5/ Here's the mistake most PLG teams make with activation:

They define it as a time-based or count threshold. Logged in 3x in 7 days. Completed 3 actions. Uploaded a file.

That tells you a user touched your product. It doesn't tell you they got anything from it.

6/ Kyle Poyar put it well: "Activation is more of a score than a binary yes/no, and conversion increases dramatically as the score goes up."

The aha moment isn't a checkbox. It's a pattern of behaviors tied to your core value.

7/ The most revealing stat in all of PLG right now:

Only 34% of PLG companies actively track activation as a metric.

2 in 3 PLG teams are running a growth motion without measuring the thing that most predicts whether a free user ever pays.

8/ Why? Activation sits at the intersection of product, marketing, and customer success.

In most orgs, nobody owns the 4-14 days between signup and value. Product owns the feature. Marketing owns the signup. CS owns the "at risk" user.

Nobody owns the gap.

9/ Buyer behavior is making this more expensive by the day.

A March 2026 Gartner survey found 67% of B2B buyers now prefer a rep-free experience for at least part of their purchase journey.

Your product's first session IS the sales call. It either activates them or it doesn't.

10/ The fix that actually works: product qualified lead scoring.

Companies using PQL frameworks see 25-30% free-to-paid conversion. Companies routing unscored leads see 5-10%.

That's a 3x difference from one structural change in how the GTM team decides who gets human attention.

11/ And yet only 25% of PLG companies have a PQL framework.

The other 75% are still qualifying leads by company size and job title — firmographic signals from the sales-led era — while sitting on a PLG motion.

12/ The actual fix starts at product architecture, not the email queue.

What does a user need to experience in their first session to feel the product is for them? That answer becomes your activation design target.

Minimize the distance between signup and that moment. Everything else follows.

13/ The teams that win the next generation of PLG aren't the ones with the best acquisition machine.

They're the ones who figured out how to make every free user feel like a paying customer before they ever open their wallet.
