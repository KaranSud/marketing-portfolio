Give a rep a list of two hundred accounts and most will start at the top and work down. That is the same as working at random. The accounts that deserve your first hour are rarely the ones that happen to be alphabetically first. A simple fit score fixes this.

## Fit is not the same as size

It is tempting to rank by company size and chase the biggest logos. Size is one input, not the answer. A huge company with no reason to buy this quarter is a worse use of your time than a mid-sized one with an obvious, current need. Fit is about the match between your offer and their situation right now.

## The signals that actually predict fit

A useful score blends a few honest signals, each grounded in something real rather than a gut feel.

- **Profile match.** Are they the kind of business your offer is built for, in industry, size, and model.
- **Timing triggers.** Are they hiring for the problem you solve, launching something, entering a market, or recently funded. Timing often matters more than fit on paper.
- **Reachability.** Can you actually find the right person and a way to contact them. A perfect-fit account you cannot reach is not workable yet.
- **Evidence of the pain.** Is there a visible sign they have the problem your offer addresses, not just an assumption that they might.

You do not need a data science model. A score from zero to a hundred, built from a handful of these signals, is enough to sort the list into work-now, work-later, and skip.

## Be honest with the score

The discipline that makes scoring useful is honesty. If everything scores high, the score is worthless. Most accounts on a cold list are a weak or moderate fit, and only a few are a clear, timely match. A good score uses the full range and is willing to say that an account is a poor fit, so you can drop it and move on.

## What ranking buys you

When the list is sorted by real fit, the math of outbound changes. The same hours go to the accounts most likely to reply and close. The weak-fit accounts stop quietly draining your week. And because the top of the list is genuinely relevant, your messaging gets sharper, because it is easy to be specific about an account that actually fits.

Work the best-fit accounts first. It sounds obvious, and almost nobody does it, which is exactly why it is an edge.
